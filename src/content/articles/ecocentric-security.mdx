## Axiom

> Security cannot be reduced to military capacity. It is a relationship between people, nature, and society.

The debate about security is trapped in **anthropocentrism** — it concerns only the survival of the nation-state, the preservation of borders, the projection of human power. But the foundation beneath the nation-state is not military — it is ecological. Without functioning ecosystems, there is no agriculture, no clean water, no breathable air, and therefore **no civilization to defend.**

Arne Naess's ecosophy teaches us that nature's integrity is not a luxury that can be sacrificed when security demands it. **Nature's integrity is the precondition of all security.**

---

## The Invisible Sacrifice

The shift is already underway, and it is quiet. Funds originally earmarked for climate action are being **silently redirected**:

- Offshore wind is no longer about saving the biosphere — it's about "energy security in wartime"
- Seabed mineral extraction is no longer about batteries for electric vehicles — it's about "independence from China"
- Arctic research is no longer about understanding melting ice caps — it's about "northern flank strategy"

When sustainability is sacrificed on the altar of security, we lose sight of a fundamental truth: **science's true mission is ensuring humanity's long-term survival,** not optimizing the short-term military advantage of any single nation.

---

## The Ecological Cost of War

War does not merely destroy human lives. It destroys the **web of life.**

- 75,000 trees were sacrificed in a Polish forest for a single NATO training exercise
- In Ukraine, bombed stables leave animals starving, habitats are destroyed, endangered species lose their last refuges
- Radioactive fallout and chemical pollution know no national borders
- The manufacturing of weapons systems consumes vast quantities of rare earth minerals, leaving behind toxic wastelands

The WHO classifies violence as a contagious disease. By the same epidemiological logic, **war is an ecological pandemic** — a cascading system failure that propagates through every living network it touches.

---

## The Opportunity Cost

Dwight D. Eisenhower — who understood war better than most — framed it precisely:

> *"Every gun that is made, every warship launched, every rocket fired signifies, in the final sense, a theft from those who hunger and are not fed, those who are cold and are not clothed."*

Every hour a top researcher spends optimizing a missile guidance system is an hour **not spent** developing drought-resistant crops, new antibiotics, or atmospheric carbon capture. Every billion spent on a frigate is a billion not spent on reforestation, ocean cleanup, or pandemic preparedness.

We have detailed military maps of the seabed for submarine warfare, but we lack basic knowledge of the ecosystems we are destroying. **We know how to annihilate an enemy, but precious little about how to keep the foundation of life intact.**

What will we defend when the war is over, if the biosphere is dead?

---

## Deep Ecology as Defense Doctrine

Naess's **Deep Ecology Platform** offers a radically different security framework:

1. The well-being of non-human life has value **independent of its usefulness** to human purposes
2. The richness and diversity of life forms contribute to the realization of these values and are values in themselves
3. Humans have no right to reduce this richness except to satisfy **vital needs**
4. Present human interference with the non-human world is excessive, and the situation is worsening

Applied to defense: a security doctrine that destroys the biosphere in order to protect the state is **logically incoherent.** It secures the container while destroying the contents.

---

## The Whole Defense

A total defense worthy of the name must include:

- **Ecological resilience** — functioning ecosystems as the foundation of food, water, and air security
- **Social resilience** — welfare systems, healthcare, education, and trust as the immune system against hybrid threats
- **Intellectual resilience** — free research directed at existential threats, not locked into military path dependence
- **Nonviolent resistance capacity** — civilian defense infrastructure based on Sharp's and Chenoweth's documented methods

A society where nursing homes are crumbling, where the disabled live in poverty, where youth fall out of education — that society is **easy to destabilize** regardless of how many frigates it owns.

Total defense begins in the kindergarten, the hospital, the forest, and the ocean. **It ends — if anywhere — at the military base.**

---

## Declaration

We refuse the false choice between security and sustainability. We refuse to sacrifice the biosphere for a defense doctrine that was obsolete before it was built.

The first and final line of defense is not a frigate. It is **the living Earth.**
